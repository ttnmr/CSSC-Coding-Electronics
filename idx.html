Project Eris

In January 2005, The Palomar Observatory discovered Eris. Eris is the second largest known dwarf planet. It was named after the Greek goddess of contention and rivalry. Eris is approximately ⅕ the width of Earth, spanning a diameter of 1,500 miles.

It’s unlikely Eris contains any life forms, as it consists of mainly rocky material and reaches extremely cold temperatures. Its estimated that surface temperatures reach around -217 to -243 degrees celsius.

By the means of our project, we’ve created an experimentation probe to simulate documenting data on Eris.
